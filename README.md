## Business Problem Statement

The company recently introduced a new onboarding and activation campaign with the goal of helping more users successfully move from signup to paid subscription. 
What decision needs to be made?
Before rolling out this new experience to all users, leadership wants to understand whether it delivers meaningful business value compared to the current onboarding process.

Who the decision impacts?
This decision will impact several teams, including Product, Marketing, Customer Success and Revenue Operations, as a full rollout would influence customer acquisition, user experience, support workload and overall revenue growth.

What metric should improve?
The primary objective of this analysis is to determine whether the new onboarding experience increases paid conversion rates and improves early user engagement. However, a higher conversion rate alone is not enough to justify a launch.

What risks must be monitored?
The company must also ensure that the new experience does not create unintended negative consequences such as increased support requests, higher refund rates, or poorer experiences for certain user segments.

What evidence is required before making a recommendation?
To make an informed recommendation, the experiment results must provide clear statistical evidence that the treatment group performs better than the control group while maintaining acceptable performance across key guardrail metrics.
